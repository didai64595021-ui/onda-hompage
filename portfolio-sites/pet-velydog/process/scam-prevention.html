Scam Prevention

The online pet industry unfortunately attracts fraudulent sellers who prey on families looking for their perfect companion. At Velydog, we believe in transparency and want to help you make safe, informed decisions. Here is everything you need to know about avoiding pet scams and verifying legitimate breeders.

Warning Signs

Red Flags to Watch For

Prices Too Good to Be True

Scammers often list puppies at significantly below-market prices to attract victims. Premium teacup puppies from reputable breeders have consistent pricing that reflects the quality of care, health testing, and ethical breeding practices.

Cryptocurrency or Gift Card Payments

Legitimate breeders never ask for payment via cryptocurrency, gift cards, or untraceable methods. These payment methods offer zero buyer protection and are a hallmark of fraudulent operations.

No Video Calls or Live Photos

If a seller refuses to do a live video call or can only provide stock photos, it is a major red flag. Reputable breeders are happy to show you the actual puppy and their facility in real time.

Pressure to Act Quickly

Scammers create urgency by claiming other buyers are interested or that the price will increase soon. Legitimate breeders understand this is a big decision and give you time to make it.

No Physical Address or Contact

Real breeders have a physical location and are transparent about their address, phone number, and business details. Be wary of sellers who only communicate through email or messaging apps with no verifiable identity.

No Health Guarantee

Reputable breeders always offer a health guarantee and provide complete vaccination and health records. If a seller cannot provide these, or makes vague promises, look elsewhere.

Stay Safe

Payment Protection Tips

Follow these guidelines to protect yourself when purchasing a puppy online, whether from Velydog or any other breeder.

01Always verify the seller's identity and business registration before sending any payment
02Request a live video call to see the specific puppy you are interested in
03Ask for references from previous buyers and check online reviews
04Verify the seller's physical address and contact information independently
05Use secure payment methods that offer buyer protection (PayPal, bank transfer)
06Never send payment via cryptocurrency, wire transfer to personal accounts, or gift cards
07Ask for a written health guarantee and review the terms before purchasing
08Be cautious of sellers who refuse to provide veterinary records or health certificates
09Research typical pricing for the breed you want; unrealistically low prices are a warning sign
10Trust your instincts. If something feels off, walk away and keep looking
